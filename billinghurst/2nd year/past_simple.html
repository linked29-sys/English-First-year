The Past Simple (Pret√©rito Simple) is used to talk about completed actions in the past. For example:

They watch (mirar) television every evening. (Present Simple)

They watched (miraron) television yesterday evening. (Past Simple)

To form the Past Simple for regular verbs, add -ed to the base form. This applies to I/we/you/they/he/she/it.

Tip for better understanding: Focus on the time expressions like "yesterday" or "last week" to identify when to use Past Simple. This will improve your score in grammar tests by recognizing context quickly.
